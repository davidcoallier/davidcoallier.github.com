If PRISM Had to Raise Money

Jun 12th, 2013

Introduction

For those of you who are not aware of PRISM, it is a clandestine national security electronic surveillance program that has been ran by the National Security Agency since 2007.

In an attempt to remain neutral, I’m going to say that it has caused much confusion and anger in the online community. Operating under incomplete information, I will reserve my judgement on the matter until supplementary facts come out.

Being someone with a sense of humour, I thought it would be funny to slip into PRISMs’ shoes for a minute and see how it would feel if PRISM were to be a startup attempting to raise money.

Investor Pitch Deck

My rules for entrepreneurs creating investor decks are pretty simple. Only a few slides have to be present:

  • The product presentation,
  • The Problem Description,
  • The Solution to The Problem,
  • The Market Validation,
  • The Market Size,
  • The Product details,
  • The Business and Revenue Model,
  • The Adoption and Expansion Plan,
  • The Competition and Competitive Advantage,
  • The Ask (How much you’re looking for).

The goal is to allow anyone who isn’t in the room to catch a glimpse of your potential without having to read a full business plan (My views on business plans are also quite opinionated).
